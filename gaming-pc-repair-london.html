FAQ

Gaming PC & Console Repair FAQs

A PC that won't power on could be caused by a faulty PSU, a failed motherboard, a short circuit from a component, or even a loose power cable. Bring it in for a free diagnostic and we'll identify the exact cause. Common fixes include PSU replacement, reseating components, or in rare cases, motherboard repair or replacement.

Overheating is most commonly caused by dried-out thermal paste on the CPU or GPU, dust buildup blocking heatsinks and fan intakes, failed or worn fans, or insufficient case airflow. A cooling system overhaul — which includes a deep clean, new thermal paste, and fan inspection — typically resolves overheating issues completely.

Once all components are sourced and available, the physical build typically takes 2–4 hours, followed by testing, Windows setup, and driver installation. From your initial consultation to collecting your finished PC, you can typically expect 3–7 days depending on component availability. We keep you updated throughout the process.

If your PS5 is within Sony's warranty period, we recommend contacting Sony first as a third-party repair may affect your manufacturer warranty. However, if your console is out of warranty — which applies to most consoles we see, especially PS4 and older Xbox models — we offer the best-value repair option with our own 90-day parts warranty.

Absolutely — this is one of our favourite things to do! Tell us your budget, the games you play, and what you want to achieve (high FPS, 4K, streaming, etc.) and we'll put together the perfect spec. We know every component in the market and will advise you honestly on where to spend and where to save to get the most performance for your budget.

GPU artifacting refers to visual glitches — random coloured pixels, screen tearing, corrupted geometry — that appear during gaming or general use. It can be caused by overheating (dried thermal pads), driver issues, or VRAM failure. We diagnose the specific cause and carry out the appropriate repair, which could be a thermal repaste, driver fix, or in some cases a GPU replacement.
